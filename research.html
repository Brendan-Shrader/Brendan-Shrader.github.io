Mathematical Epidemiology

I began researching mathematical epidemiology in the summer of 2023 as a part of the University of Central Florida's (UCF) NSF-funded Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) in Applied and Computational Mathematics under Prof. Zhisheng Shuai. I carried out analysis on the ordinary differential equation (ODE) compartmental SIR model depicted in Fig 1. I proved the uniqueness and stability of the disease-free and endemic equilibria, and proved global stability for the disease-free equilibirum.

This lead to my Honors Undergraduate Thesis (HUT), also mentored by Zhisheng, where I quantified the effects of important parameters on long-term outbreak outcomes and generalized my method of proving the global stability of the disease-free equilibrium. This led to several presentations, including ones at JMM 2024, SMB MathEpiOnco 2024, and JMM 2025.

Bacteria Population Dynamics

In the summer of 2024, I attended Georiga Tech's (GT) Mathematics REU, where I explored and analyze multiple ODE models of bacteria population growth and communication under the mentorship of Profs. Rachel Kuske (GT Math) and Sam Brown (GT Biosciences).
