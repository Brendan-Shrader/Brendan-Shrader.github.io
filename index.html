Hello, I'm Brendan

Welcome to my website! I am an incoming student in Georgia Tech's Quantitative Biosciences (QBioS) PhD program.

I completed a B.S. in Mathematics at the University of Central Florida in May 2025, with a minor in computer science. I am generally interested in mathematical biology, differential equations, advanced calculus, and the connections between them. I have participated in two NSF-funded undergraduate research programs, with topics ranging from epidemiological modeling to bacterial population dynamics (GT Math REU 2024, UCF Math REU 2023). My UCF Honors Undergraduate Thesis, mentored by Prof. Zhisheng Shuai, examines an SIR epidemic model to understand 1) the role of incidence rates in producing periodic epidemics, 2) the impact of partial immunity on disease endemicity, and 3) the utility of rational Lyapunov functions to prove the global stability of disease-free equilibria.

While I'm originally from Long Island, NY, I grew up in Jacksonville, FL. I spend my free time socializing, working out (swimming, weightlifting), playing the piano, or playing video games.

Academic Interests

  • Mathematical biology - population dynamics, epidemiology, bacterial communication
  • Differential equations - ordinary, partial, and stochastic to model biological processes
  • Advanced calculus - real analysis, integration, measures & probability theory, differential geometry
